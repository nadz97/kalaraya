Modern Tropical Villa

Designing a home in the tropics requires a deep understanding of the local climate, culture, and material availability. For this project, our goal was to create a sanctuary that blurs the line between indoor and outdoor living, maximizing natural ventilation while shielding the inhabitants from the intense tropical sun.

Client

Mr. & Mrs. Heryanto

Location

Pontianak, West Kalimantan

Area

450 m²

Year

2024

The Concept

We started with a simple massing layout, prioritizing the central courtyard. This void acts as the lungs of the house, circulating cool air throughout the main living spaces. Large overhangs and timber screens provide shade without compromising the view.

"Architecture should not just be about shelter, but about creating a dialogue between the built environment and nature."

The material palette is kept deliberately simple: exposed concrete, local teak wood, and natural stone. This not only roots the building in its context but also ensures it ages gracefully over time.

Sustainable Features

Sustainability was a core driver for this project. We incorporated a rainwater harvesting system, solar panels on the roof, and passive cooling strategies that reduce the need for air conditioning by 40%.

The result is a home that feels both luxurious and grounded, a testament to the potential of modern tropical architecture in urban settings.
